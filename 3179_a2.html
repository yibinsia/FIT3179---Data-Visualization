Global GDP Overview

Gross Domestic Product (GDP) measures the market value of goods and services over a time period, produced by a country or countries.

Although Europe has several leading GDP countries, the United States and China still hold the top two positions globally. The combined GDP of all top European countries does not even come close to the GDP of the United States! While many countries in Africa have comparatively lower GDPs on a global scale.

Further Analysis
Will higher GDP lead to higher life expectancies?

Human life expectancy represents an estimate of remaining years at a given age.

Oceania has the highest life expectancy, reaching around 80 years. Europe follows with about 77 years, Americas at 75 years, and Asia close to 72.5 years. Africa shows a gap, with a lower average of 60 years.

Macao from China holds the highest life expectancy worldwide. Major GDP leaders like Japan, China, United States, and Germany generally have life expectancy rates of 75-85 years. However, India, a GDP leader with a large population, has lower life expectancy, likely due to living conditions. Chad ranks the lowest worldwide, with just 53 years.

Many African countries have lower life expectancies, reflecting GDP-related limitations in healthcare, nutrition, and infrastructure access. This shows that life expectancy is generally higher in regions with higher GDPs, and Oceania countries could be the most developed in terms of healthcare and living conditions.
